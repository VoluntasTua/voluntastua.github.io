On Ego

There’s a misconception that the ego is bad. The worst thing a spiritual person could be is egoistic. But what is meant by “ego?” The word came into English by way of Freud’s translator. Freud put forth a tripartite model of mind consisting of “the It”, “the I”, and “the Over-I”. To make Freud sound more scholarly, his English translator preferred the Latin: Id, Ego, and Superego. In Freud’s conception, the “I”, or ego, is not bad. It’s simply the part you associate with yourself. None of them are bad, though we often treat “the it” as if it were, dissociating ourselves from “it”.

So if ego is just the Latin word for “I”, how did it get a bad rap? Spiritual thinkers say the goal is to transcend the ego. So far as this line of thinking goes, I am in agreement with it.

Unfortunately, something got lost once it hit the mainstream. The goal of transcending the ego got interpreted to mean the ego is “bad.” This is absurd.

It’s like wings transcending wheels. Once a plane takes off, the wheels are useless. But if a plane didn’t have any wheels, you would be concerned. How will it take off? A plane without wheels can’t take off. Just as we need wheels in order to transcend the need for wheels, so do we need an ego, and a well developed one at that, in order to transcend it.
